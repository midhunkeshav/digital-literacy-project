## General Online Safety

1. Verify email sender addresses carefully – Always check the full domain.
   "exams@vitbhopal-portal.co.in" is NOT the same as "exams@vitbhopal.ac.in"

2. Never click suspicious links – If an email asks you to "verify your account
   urgently", go directly to the official website by typing the URL yourself.

3. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all accounts – Enable it on your
   college email, GitHub, and LinkedIn. Even if your password is stolen, your
   account stays protected.

4. Use strong unique passwords – At least 12 characters with uppercase,
   lowercase, numbers and symbols. Never reuse passwords across platforms.

5. Keep your devices and apps updated – Updates contain security patches.
   Always install them promptly.

6. Be cautious on public Wi-Fi – Avoid accessing banking apps or your college
   portal on public Wi-Fi in cafes or buses.

---

## Financial and UPI Safety

7. Never share your UPI PIN or OTP with anyone – No bank or government
   official will ever ask for your PIN over a call or message. If someone
   does, it is a scam. Disconnect immediately.

8. Verify UPI payment requests before accepting – Receiving money never
   requires you to enter your PIN. If you are asked to enter your PIN to
   receive money, you are actually sending it. This is a common scam.

---

## Report Cybercrime in India

- National Cyber Crime Portal: https://www.cybercrime.gov.in
- Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (Available 24x7 across India)
- Local Police: File an FIR at your nearest police station under IT Act 2000

Report immediately – early reporting improves chances of recovering
lost money or data.
