## What is Phishing?
Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers pretend to be a trusted organization
to steal passwords, OTPs, or bank details from victims.

## Case Study: The Fake University Portal Attack

Riya, a second-year engineering student, received an email appearing to be
from her university's exam department. The subject line read: "URGENT:
Re-verify your student portal credentials before the exam schedule is released."
The email used the university logo and looked completely official. The sender
address was "exams@vitbhopal-portal.co.in" — slightly different from the real domain.

How it happened step by step:
1. The attacker created a fake domain nearly identical to the university's real one.
2. A bulk email was sent to students during exam season when anxiety is high.
3. The email linked to a fake login page that looked exactly like the real portal.
4. Riya, worried about missing her exam schedule, entered her username and password.
5. Her credentials were instantly captured by the attacker's server.
6. Within hours the attacker locked her out and accessed her personal records.

Who is targeted:
Students, senior citizens, and employees under pressure are most commonly targeted.
Phishing campaigns are deliberately timed around exams, tax deadlines, or bank alerts.

Consequences:
- Loss of access to academic accounts
- Identity theft using leaked personal data
- Financial loss if banking details are also stolen
- Emotional distress and loss of trust in digital platforms
