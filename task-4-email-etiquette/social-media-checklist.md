# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students
## CSE0001 Digital Literacy | Midhun Keshav J T | 25BCE10589

---

## DO's

1. Keep your profiles professional – Use a clear photo and a bio that reflects
   your academic interests.

2. Share your achievements – Post about certifications, projects, and
   hackathons to build your professional brand.

3. Engage respectfully – Like and comment on content from peers and
   professionals in a constructive manner.

4. Use LinkedIn for networking – Connect with seniors, alumni, and faculty
   with a personalised connection request.

5. Think before you post – Ask yourself: Would I be comfortable if my future
   employer saw this? If not, don't post it.

6. Cite sources – When sharing articles or news, always credit the original
   author or publication.

7. Keep privacy settings updated – Regularly review who can see your posts
   and personal information.

---

## DON'Ts

1. Don't post personal information – Never share your phone number, address,
   or college ID publicly.

2. Don't engage in arguments or hate speech – Offensive comments can damage
   your reputation permanently.

3. Don't share unverified news – Always check the credibility of a story
   before sharing it.

4. Don't overshare your daily routine – Posting your exact location or travel
   plans in real-time is a safety risk.

5. Don't badmouth your college or professors – Public complaints can affect
   your references and job prospects.

6. Don't accept requests from strangers blindly – Verify who you are
   connecting with on all platforms.

7. Don't plagiarise content – Copying without credit is unethical and illegal.

---

Remember: The internet has a long memory. What you post today may be seen
by employers and professors years from now.
